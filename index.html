What is the fixed asset turnover ratio?
The fixed asset turnover (FAT) ratio is an indicator of how efficient a business uses fixed assets to generate sales.
How to calculate fixed asset turnover ratio?
To calculate fixed asset turnover ratio you should use the following formula:
fixed asset turnover ratio (FAT) = net sales / average fixed assets
example: company x has an annual sales of $21,000,000 in 2021 the net fixed assets in the beginning of the year the net fixed assets was $2,300,000, calculate the fixed asset turnover ratio?
using the fixed asset turnover ratio the answer will be:
fixed asset turnover ratio = net sales / average fixed assets
fixed asset turnover ratio = $21,000,000 / (($2,000,000 + $2,300,000) / 2)
fixed asset turnover ratio = 9.76
Why use fixed asset turnover ratio?
The fixed asset turnover ratio is used to reveal the efficiency of a company at generating sales from it's existing fixed assets.
